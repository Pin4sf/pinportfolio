# Building OneSync: The Biological Intelligence Layer for AI Agents

Every AI agent being built today — Lindy, Manus, OpenClaw — can manage your calendar and draft your emails. But none of them know when you're burning out.

## The Blind Spot

Y Combinator's 2026 RFS calls for "AI that can DO, not just assist." Nearly 50% of their latest batch is AI agents. NVIDIA GTC 2026 declared autonomous agents the next infrastructure layer. The agent economy is here.

And every single one of these agents is blind to your body.

They'll schedule your hardest meeting when your HRV has crashed. They'll push you through a 14-hour day when your biology is screaming for recovery. 62% of knowledge workers report burnout. The tools meant to help are making it worse — because they optimize what you do without understanding who you are.

## The Insight That Changed Everything

The race to build the definitive personal AI agent has a blind spot. Everyone is starting from the outside in: calendars, emails, tasks, preferences. They're building AI that knows what you do.

What you do is a performance. What your body does is the truth.

Your calendar says you have twelve focused hours. Your HRV says your nervous system has been recovering for three days. Your task list says you're on track. Your sleep data says you've accumulated six hours of debt this week. No AI today catches that gap — the gap between what you think you can do and what your body can actually sustain.

## What We're Actually Building

OneSync is an AI that reads your smartwatch and tells you each morning how to make the most of your day. It briefs you on your cognitive readiness, learns what works for YOU, tracks your stress patterns over weeks, and gets smarter every day.

```
Wearable → CRS Score → Stress Detection → Claude Agent → Proactive Message → Feedback → Learns
```

The Cognitive Readiness Score (CRS) combines sleep quality, heart rate variability, circadian rhythm, and activity data into a single 0-100 score that tells you how sharp you are RIGHT NOW. Not how you slept — how ready you are to think.

The agent reaches you on Telegram — not buried in another health app you'll forget to open. A morning brief. Real-time stress interventions. Conversational health chat when you need it.

## The Technical Architecture

We went cross-platform from day one. Apple Watch gives us beat-to-beat IBI for true HRV (the gold standard). Health Connect covers the Android ecosystem. All health data is encrypted on-device with SQLCipher — the CRS computation happens on your phone, never on our servers.

The agent runs on Claude Haiku 4.5 with a rules-based pre-filter that handles 60-80% of decisions at $0 AI cost. Only meaningful events — stress spikes, morning briefs, user messages — hit the LLM. This keeps per-user costs under $0.90/month for Pro users, giving us 79% margins.

The agent has 8 tools for MVP: read your CRS, sleep data, stress events, activity, user profile, and memory — plus write tools for updating memory and sending messages. A hook pipeline runs quality gates before and after every Claude call, ensuring the agent never sends a message it isn't confident about.

## Two Intelligence Layers

What makes this work is combining biological intelligence with life context:

**Biology** (what your body says): Wearable data — HRV, HR, sleep stages, steps, SpO2 — computed into a cognitive readiness score updated every 15 minutes.

**Life Context** (what your world looks like): Calendar, email, Slack, task managers — understanding what's coming, what's urgent, what can move.

When the agent knows both, it can do things no other tool can: "Your CRS crashed to 38 and you have a board presentation at 2pm — want me to suggest moving it to tomorrow morning when you're typically at 78?"

No health app can do that. No productivity agent can do that. Only an agent that reads both your body and your world.

## What I've Learned So Far

**Start from the body outward.** Every other agent starts from the calendar inward. That's why they all converge on the same features. Biology is the un-replicable data layer — your HRV baseline can't be copied by a competitor plugging into the same calendar API.

**Proactive is the only retention mechanism.** Health apps have 3-12% 30-day retention rates. The reason: dashboards are passive. You have to go to them. OneSync comes to you. A proactive morning brief on Telegram is fundamentally different from a score sitting in an app.

**The agent IS the product.** The app is just the cockpit. The real experience happens in a Telegram conversation where the agent knows your biology, your patterns, and your world — and acts before you ask.

**Build for trust, not engagement.** The system stays quiet when it's not confident. In a domain where false signals erode trust, showing nothing is better than a false green light. Every message passes 5 quality gates before reaching the user.
